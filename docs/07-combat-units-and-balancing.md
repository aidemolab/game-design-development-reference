# 07. Combat, Units, and Balancing

## Standard

Combat should communicate intent, impact, and outcome. Units or abilities need identifiable roles, counters, costs, and limitations.

## Lessons

- Age of Empires uses unit counters and technology choices to reward scouting and adaptation.
- OpenRA uses faction differences, build choices, positioning, and deterministic rules.
- Stronghold Crusader connects siege roles to defensive structures.
- PUBG emphasises positioning, equipment uncertainty, terrain, and escalating pressure.

## Best practices

- Define the role of every unit or weapon in one sentence.
- Balance complete strategies, not isolated statistics.
- Make damage, range, cooldown, armour, and status effects legible.
- Use playtesting data alongside observation and player feedback.
- Separate difficulty from unfairness; AI should generally follow understandable rules.
- Change one balance variable at a time and record the result.

## Counter design

Counters should create adaptation rather than automatic victory. Soft counters allow skill and context to matter; hard counters should be used carefully and communicated clearly.

## Scope warning

Begin with a very small roster. Three well-defined roles are more testable than twenty poorly differentiated units.
