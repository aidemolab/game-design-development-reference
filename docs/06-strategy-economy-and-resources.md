# 06. Strategy, Economy, and Resources

## Standard

Resources should create meaningful decisions. An economy is valuable when acquisition, conversion, storage, allocation, and loss affect strategy.

## Reference lessons

- Stronghold Crusader links raw materials, food, weapons, popularity, troops, and fortifications.
- Age of Empires makes worker allocation and technological timing central decisions.
- Albion Online links gathering, crafting, trade, equipment, risk, and player demand.
- Conflict of Nations turns production time, research, distance, and intelligence into strategic resources.
- Mindustry integrates extraction, transport, manufacturing, defence, and expansion.

## Best practices

- Give each resource a distinct purpose.
- Make conversion chains understandable through UI and feedback.
- Create opportunity cost: spending on one plan delays another.
- Avoid redundant resources that differ only by name.
- Introduce complexity progressively.
- Provide recovery routes so one early mistake does not always decide the entire game.

## Balancing questions

- What enters and leaves the economy?
- Can resources accumulate without meaningful use?
- Is one strategy dominant in most situations?
- Are bottlenecks visible and understandable?
- Can the player anticipate the consequence of an investment?
